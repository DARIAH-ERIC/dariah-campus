Massimiliano Carloni is a data modeller and repository manager at the Austrian Centre for Digital Humanities and Cultural Heritage (ACDH-CH). His main interests lie in long-term digital preservation and semantic technologies, with a special focus on graph-based data models and Linked Open Data. He is part of the managing team behind the archive for digital research data ARCHE at the ACDH-CH and has contributed to the creation of the new library catalogue of the Austrian Academy of Sciences. He is currently working in the project RepoLandscape, which aims to harmonise several digital repositories across Austrian institutions. Since 2021 he has been collaborating in the research project INDIGO, where he is responsible for the digital archiving of the data and the development of a thesaurus to describe contemporary graffiti.
