This resource explores the challenges of validation within complex spatial simulation projects. Drawing on practical examples connected to ARTEMIS activities, the session discusses how complexity emerges from interconnected data flows, historical accuracy requirements, simulation layers, and collaborative workflows. 

Particular emphasis is placed on the importance of strategic validation, defining clear project boundaries, and maintaining focus on the original project objectives despite evolving assumptions, scope expansion, and technical challenges. The session also discusses practical approaches such as mapping actors and data flows, validating from the end-user perspective, and integrating semantic knowledge structures to support reliable, scalable, and research-driven spatial simulations.

## Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, learners will be able to: 

* evaluate the risks of unmanaged complexity.
* identify common sources of complexity in spatial simulation and digital heritage projects 
* analyze how different layers of the simulation influence the accuracy and reliability of the immersive environment.
* assess the importance of user-centered validation approaches in immersive simulation environments.
