The respectful and ethical treatment of the subjects of data has long been upheld, but the principles for best practice around data drawn from indigenous peoples requires particular attention.  In this presentation, Prof. Dan O'Donnell from the University of Lethbridge (Canada) discusses the CARE Principles, and how they have helped to address issues in his own research among small communities.

This presentation was recorded as part of the DARIAH Friday Frontiers in-house webinar series.

## Learning Outcomes

After viewing this video, you will:

- understand what the CARE Principles are and what they stand for
- recognise how the CARE Principles can assist with issues that may arise while conducting research among small communities
- recognise and be able to apply best practice when dealing with data drawn from indigenous peoples
